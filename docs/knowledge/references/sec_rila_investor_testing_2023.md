# Regulatory Summary: SEC RILA Investor Testing Report (2023)

**Tier**: L3 (Regulatory Research)
**Citation**: Carman, K., Chin, A., Cook, J., Nash, S. B., Scholl, B., & Zimmerman, D. B. (2023). Investor Testing Report on Registered Index-Linked Annuities. OIAD Working Paper 2023-01. U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission.
**Status**: [x] Acquired | [x] Summarized

---

## Key Purpose

Research study fulfilling Congressional mandate (Consolidated Appropriations Act 2022) to:
1. Engage in investor testing for RILAs
2. Incorporate results into SEC disclosure form design
3. Ensure key information is conveyed in terms purchasers can understand

---

## Study Components

### 1. Market Simulation Analysis
- Historical performance simulation of RILA investment options
- Analyzed how insurance features affect returns under various market conditions

### 2. Marketing Review
- Examined how RILAs are presented to potential purchasers

### 3. Qualitative Testing
- 20 in-depth interviews with annuity owners and shoppers
- Open-ended exploration of comprehension barriers

### 4. Quantitative Testing
- ~2,500 participants
- Tested variants of Key Information Table (KIT) disclosure
- Examined different introductory texts

## RILA Structure Summary

### Contract vs Investment Option

| Level | Duration | Features |
|-------|----------|----------|
| **Contract** | Multi-year (5-10+) | Surrender charges, tax penalties |
| **Investment Option** | 1-6 years | Index, insurance features, interim value adjustment |

**Critical insight**: Confusion between these levels was a primary barrier to investor understanding.

### Insurance Features

| Feature | Description | Effect |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| **Buffer** | Absorbs losses up to threshold | e.g., 20% buffer → insurer absorbs first 20% loss |
| **Floor** | Maximum loss percentage | e.g., 10% floor → investor loses at most 10% |
| **Cap** | Maximum gain percentage | e.g., 8% cap → gains limited to 8% |
| **Participation Rate** | % of index return credited | e.g., 80% participation → 8% credited if index +10% |

## Key Findings

### 1. Simulation Analysis

#### Finding 1.1: Insurance Features Create Complex Trade-offs

**Observation**:
- Caps significantly limit returns relative to underlying index
- Downside protections may avert relative losses
- **But benefits should not be overstated**

**Critical issue**: Identical insurance features produced widely varying outcomes depending on **when** investment occurred due to **timing risk**.

#### Finding 1.2: Some RILA Options May Increase Risk

> "We identified investment options that increase certain risk measures in historical simulations despite the products largely being sold as risk mitigation instruments." (p. 57)

**Implication**: Some investment options may not operate as consumers expect.

#### Finding 1.3: Term Structure Complicates Performance

**Example**: A RILA contract with two 3-year terms ≠ single 6-year term
- Mid-stream value recalculation
- Forced realization of gains/losses at term end
- "Timing risk" unique to RILAs vs mutual funds

### 2. Marketing Review Findings

#### Finding 2.1: Overwhelming Complexity

> "The number of choices an investor needs to make when investing in a RILA... could easily be overwhelming." (p. 58)

**Choices required**:
- Insurance provider
- Underlying index
- Insurance features (buffer, floor, cap, participation)
- Term length
- Investment options allocation

#### Finding 2.2: Information Gap

Marketing materials focus on **whether** to consider a RILA but often omit:
- Information for **which specific options** to choose
- Pros/cons of different menu items
- Decision-relevant comparative information

### 3. Qualitative Testing Findings

#### Finding 3.1: Terminology Confusion

**Major issue**: "Term" or "investment term"

| Investor interpretation | Actual meaning |
|------------------------|----------------|
| Duration locked into RILA contract | Duration locked into specific investment option |

**Consequence**: Misdirected interpretations of other features.

#### Finding 3.2: Feature Confusion

**Highly confusing concepts**:
- Buffer mechanics
- Interim Value Adjustment (IVA)
- Distinction between contract and investment option

> "Even these [sophisticated] individuals demonstrated confusion or lost interest in RILAs once that confusion was abated." (p. 59)

#### Finding 3.3: Graphics Did Not Help

**Observation**: Graphical and tabular illustrations tested were not effective at improving comprehension.

**Caveat**: Specific designs tested; other designs might work better.

### 4. Quantitative Testing Findings

#### Finding 4.1: No Significant Differences Among Treatment Conditions

| Condition | Mean Comprehension |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Decision focus intro | 60.2% |
| Key terms intro | 58.5% |
| Benefits only intro | 58.7% |
| Control (no intro) | 59.1% |
| Non-Q&A format | 58.8% |
| Q&A format | 59.4% |

**Result**: "There were no significant differences, on average, among any of the treatment conditions." (p. 59)

#### Finding 4.2: Sub-Score Analysis

| Conceptual Area | Mean Comprehension |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Appropriateness | 57.1-60.1% |
| How a RILA Works | **48-53%** |
| Liquidity | 61-63% |
| Insurance Features | 56-58% |

**Critical finding**: "How a RILA Works" comprehension (~49%) **not different from random guessing**.

#### Finding 4.3: Decision Focus Introduction Showed Promise

For "Appropriateness" sub-score:
- Decision focus intro: 60.1%
- Key terms intro: 55.5% (4.5 pp decrease, p=0.005)
- Control: 56.8% (3.3 pp decrease, p=0.04)

## Economics of RILAs: Key Concerns

### 1. Timing Risk

> "An identical set of insurance features was associated with a wide variety of investment outcomes... depending on when the investment occurred." (p. 58)

**Mechanism**: RILAs force realization at term end vs. flexible sale timing for mutual funds.

### 2. Risk Mitigation May Be Overstated

**Some RILA options**:
- Increased probability of loss
- Increased magnitude of loss
- While substantially reducing upside gain

### 3. Information Scarcity

> "The scarcity of information regarding past RILA performance presented enormous challenges to our research." (p. 61)

**Impact**: Difficult to identify decision-relevant information for disclosure design.

## Recommendations for Disclosure Design

### 1. Clearer Contract vs Term Distinction

Investors need to understand:
- Typical/anticipated contract length
- Minimum contract duration for penalty-free withdrawal
- Difference between contract and investment option term

### 2. Financial Implications of Insurance Features

Need better illustration of:
- Premium invested → money out relationship
- How relationships vary in different market conditions
- Performance over contract life (not just single term)

### 3. Investment Alternatives Comparison

Comparative information against:
- Underlying index performance
- Plausible alternative investments

### 4. Early Withdrawal Consequences

Quantify costs of:
- Interim Value Adjustments
- Surrender charges
- Tax penalties (before age 59½)

### 5. Risk Information

More direct discussion of:
- Historical probability of negative return (RILA vs index)
- Likelihood and magnitude of risks
- Risk definitions investors actually use

## Key Takeaways for Product Design

### 1. Complexity is a Feature, Not a Bug (for Issuers)

The complexity creates:
- Dependence on financial professionals
- Information asymmetry favoring issuers
- Difficulty comparing products

### 2. Investor Protection Concerns

> "Investors may need additional information and assistance to understand these products' likely financial outcomes." (p. 60)

### 3. Disclosure Alone May Be Insufficient

> "The limited effects of the alternative introductory texts suggest that disclosure variants may not be a complete solution." (p. 60)

### 4. Timing Risk is Underappreciated

Unique to RILAs vs other investments:
- Forced realization at term end
- Mid-stream evaluation affects ultimate value
- No flexibility to "wait out" down markets
